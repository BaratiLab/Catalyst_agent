## Crystal Structure Formulas for ORR Catalysts

### **Perovskite Oxides (ABO₃)**

**Simple Perovskites:**

- **LaMnO₃** - Shows high ORR activity with optimal eg filling[^1_1][^1_2]
- **LaFeO₃** - Demonstrated catalytic activity for ORR[^1_2][^1_1]
- **LaCrO₃** - Exhibits moderate ORR performance[^1_1][^1_2]
- **LaCoO₃** - High activity perovskite catalyst[^1_3]
- **LaNiO₃** - Good ORR performance with eg ≈ 1[^1_3]
- **SrTiO₃** - Oxygen vacancy formation enhances catalytic properties[^1_4][^1_5]
- **CaTiO₃** - Oxygen vacancy defects provide active sites[^1_6]
- **BaTiO₃** - Enhanced oxygen diffusion properties[^1_7][^1_4]

**Mixed A-Site Perovskites:**

- **La₀.₆Ca₀.₄CoO₃₋δ** - Active for hydrogen peroxide reduction[^1_8]
- **Ba₀.₅Sr₀.₅Co₀.₈Fe₀.₂O₃₋δ (BSCF)** - Highly active OER/ORR catalyst[^1_9][^1_8]
- **SrCo₀.₈Fe₀.₂O₃₋δ (SCF)** - Comparable activity to BSCF[^1_9]
- **LaCr₀.₂₅Fe₀.₂₅Co₀.₅O₃₋δ** - Synergistic Cr-Fe-Co interactions[^1_10]


### **Double Perovskites (A₂BB'O₆)**

**Lanthanide-Based Double Perovskites:**

- **La₁.₅Sr₀.₅NiMn₀.₅Ru₀.₅O₆ (LSNMR)** - Outstanding bifunctional ORR/OER performance[^1_11][^1_12]
- **PrBaCo₂O₅₊δ** - High OER activity comparable to BSCF[^1_9]
- **SmBaCo₂O₅₊δ** - Active double perovskite structure[^1_9]
- **GdBaCo₂O₅₊δ** - Enhanced catalytic performance[^1_9]
- **HoBaCo₂O₅₊δ** - High activity double perovskite[^1_9]
- **Ba₂Bi₀.₁Sc₀.₂Co₁.₇O₆₋ₓ (BBSC)** - Low polarization resistance for ORR[^1_13][^1_14]

**Metal-Rich Double Perovskites:**

- **Sr₂FeMoO₆** - Half-metallic properties beneficial for catalysis[^1_15][^1_16]
- **Sr₂CrFeO₆** - Theoretical candidate with interesting electronic structure[^1_16]
- **Ca₂FeMoO₆** - Electronic structure favorable for oxygen reactions[^1_15]
- **Ca₂FeReO₆** - High hybridization between metal and oxygen bands[^1_15]


### **Triple Perovskites**

- **Nd₁.₅Ba₁.₅CoFeMnO₉₋δ (NBCFM)** - Superior activity and stability[^1_17]


### **Metal Oxides**

**Transition Metal Oxides:**

- **Co₃O₄** - High catalytic activity, multiple oxidation states[^1_18][^1_19]
- **Fe₂O₃** - Active for oxygen reduction with proper morphology[^1_20][^1_18]
- **NiO** - Good conductivity and catalytic properties[^1_18][^1_20]
- **MnO₂** - Multiple valence states and oxygen vacancies[^1_19][^1_21]
- **CuO** - Shape-dependent catalytic activity[^1_18]
- **ZnO** - Forms oxygen vacancies enhancing activity[^1_22]

**Mixed Metal Oxides:**

- **NiO/Co₃O₄** - Synergistic effects enhance ORR activity[^1_23][^1_20]
- **Fe₂O₃/Co₃O₄** - Enhanced catalytic performance[^1_20][^1_23]
- **Co₃O₄/MnO₂** - Excellent ORR catalytic performances[^1_19]
- **CuO/MnO₂** - Interface effects improve ORR activity[^1_24][^1_25]
- **ZnO/MnO₂** - Enhanced oxygen adsorption and reduction[^1_26]

**Rare Earth and Other Oxides:**

- **CeO₂** - Oxygen storage capacity and redox properties[^1_27][^1_28]
- **TiO₂** - High stability and strong metal-support interactions[^1_28]
- **ZrO₂** - Good support material with oxygen vacancy formation[^1_28]
- **RuO₂** - Highly active for oxygen evolution and reduction[^1_29][^1_30]
- **IrO₂** - Second most active catalyst after RuO₂[^1_31][^1_29]

**p-Block Metal Oxides:**

- **BiVO₄** - Good visible light absorption and catalytic activity[^1_32][^1_33]
- **WO₃** - Excellent electrical conductivity and stability[^1_34][^1_32]
- **In₂O₃** - Transparent conducting oxide with catalytic potential[^1_35][^1_36]
- **Ga₂O₃** - Enhanced oxygen adsorption properties[^1_37][^1_35]
- **SnO₂** - Good electrical properties for catalysis[^1_36][^1_35]
- **Cu₂O** - p-type semiconductor with oxygen reduction activity[^1_38][^1_36]


### **Spinel Ferrites (AB₂O₄)**

- **CoFe₂O₄** - Inverse spinel with good magnetic and catalytic properties[^1_39][^1_40]
- **NiFe₂O₄** - Fully inverse spinel structure[^1_41][^1_39]
- **MnFe₂O₄** - Random disordered spinel with catalytic activity[^1_42][^1_39]
- **ZnFe₂O₄** - Normal spinel structure[^1_39]


### **Ruddlesden-Popper Phases (A₂BO₄₊δ)**

- **La₂NiO₄₊δ** - High oxygen ion conductivity and interstitial oxygen[^1_43][^1_44]
- **Sr₂FeO₄** - Layered structure with oxygen transport properties[^1_45]
- **La₂CoO₄₊δ** - Enhanced oxygen exchange kinetics[^1_45]


### **Multiferroic and Complex Oxides**

- **BiFeO₃** - Perovskite multiferroic with photocatalytic activity[^1_46][^1_47]
- **PbTiO₃** - Ferroelectric perovskite with unique properties[^1_48][^1_49]
- **KNbO₃** - Ferroelectric oxide with oxygen vacancy formation[^1_50][^1_46]


### **Solid Solutions and Doped Systems**

- **WO₃/BiVO₄** - Heterojunction with enhanced photoelectrochemical activity[^1_33][^1_34]
- **Sr₀.₅Ba₀.₅TiO₃** - Enhanced surface reducibility[^1_5][^1_51]
- **Ru₀.₅Ir₀.₅O₂** - Solid solution combining RuO₂ activity with IrO₂ stability[^1_29]

These materials offer a wide range of structural diversity and electronic properties that could potentially provide OH adsorption energies in your target range of 0.9-1.1 eV relative to H₂O and H₂. The specific adsorption energies will depend on the surface orientation, defect concentration, and local coordination environment of the active sites. Many of these materials have demonstrated experimental ORR activity, while others are promising theoretical candidates based on their electronic structures and oxygen handling properties.
